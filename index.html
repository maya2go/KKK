The Spirit and Origin

The Chinese tea ceremony is not merely a technique for drinking tea, but a lifestyle of cultivating the mind and character. Originating in the Tang Dynasty, it was first systematized by the "Tea Sage" Lu Yu in his book The Classic of Tea. The ceremony embodies traditional Chinese philosophical thoughts of Confucianism, Buddhism, and Taoism. It emphasizes the "harmony between humanity and nature," pursuing inner peace and spiritual elevation through the process of brewing and drinking tea.

Classic Brewing Process (Gongfu Tea)

Among Chinese tea ceremonies, the "Gongfu Tea" method is the most meticulous, filled with a deep sense of ritual. Here are the classic brewing steps:

  • 1. White Crane Bathing (Washing the cups): Scald the teaware with boiling water. This not only cleans the cups but also raises their temperature to better release the tea's aroma.
  • 2. Oolong Entering the Palace (Placing the tea): Gently use a tea scoop to place the tea leaves into the Yixing clay teapot or Gaiwan (lidded bowl).
  • 3. Suspended Pot High Pour (Pouring the water): Raise the kettle high while pouring water to let the tea leaves tumble inside the pot, ensuring an even infusion.
  • 4. Spring Breeze Brushing the Face (Skimming the foam): Use the lid of the teapot to gently skim off the foam on the surface, keeping the tea clear.
  • 5. Lord Guan Patrolling the City (Pouring the tea): Pour the tea in a continuous circular motion into the small teacups to ensure the concentration is consistent across all cups.
  • 6. Han Xin Sorting the Troops (Distributing the drops): The last drops in the teapot are often the strongest and should be evenly distributed drop by drop into each cup.
